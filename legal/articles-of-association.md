## Part 2 — Directors

### 4. Directors' general authority

Subject to the Articles, the directors are responsible for the management of the Company's business, for which purpose they may exercise all the powers of the Company.

### 5. Members' reserve power

(1) The members may, by special resolution, direct the directors to take, or refrain from taking, specified action.

(2) No such special resolution invalidates anything which the directors have done before the passing of the resolution.

### 6. Directors may delegate

(1) Subject to the Articles, the directors may delegate any of the powers which are conferred on them under the Articles to any person or committee as they think fit.

(2) The directors may revoke any delegation in whole or part, or alter its terms and conditions.

### 7. Committees

(1) Committees to which the directors delegate any of their powers must follow procedures which are based as far as they are applicable on those provisions of the Articles which govern the taking of decisions by directors.

(2) The directors may make rules of procedure for all or any committees, which prevail over rules derived from the Articles if they are not consistent with them.

## Part 3 — Board Composition and Decision-Making

### 8. Number of directors

(1) Unless otherwise determined by ordinary resolution, the number of directors shall not be less than one and not more than seven.

(2) At any time before the Company's first funding round that results in the issue of Preferred Shares, the Founder may act as sole director.

### 9. Appointment of directors

(1) Any person who is willing to act as a director, and is permitted by law to do so, may be appointed to be a director by:

- (a) ordinary resolution; or
- (b) a decision of the directors.

(2) Following the issue of Preferred Shares, the Investor Majority shall be entitled to appoint one Investor Director (and to remove and replace that Investor Director) by giving written notice to the Company.

(3) Following any funding round in which Investor Shareholders subscribe for shares representing 25% or more of the Company's issued share capital, the Investor Majority shall be entitled to appoint a second Investor Director.

(4) The Founder shall be entitled to remain a director for so long as they hold at least 10% of the issued Ordinary Shares of the Company.

### 10. Removal of directors

(1) A person ceases to be a director as soon as:

- (a) that person ceases to be a director by virtue of any provision of the Act or is prohibited from being a director by law;
- (b) a bankruptcy order is made against that person;
- (c) a registered medical practitioner gives a written opinion that the person has become physically or mentally incapable of acting as a director and may remain so for more than three months;
- (d) the person resigns by written notice to the Company;
- (e) in the case of an Investor Director, the Investor Majority gives written notice of removal.

(2) The Founder may not be removed as a director under article 168 of the Act while they hold at least 10% of the issued Ordinary Shares.

### 11. Directors' remuneration

(1) Directors may undertake any services for the Company that the directors decide.

(2) Directors are entitled to such remuneration as the directors determine for their services to the Company as directors and for any other services which they undertake for the Company.

(3) The Company may pay any reasonable expenses which the directors properly incur in connection with their attendance at meetings of directors or committees, or in connection with the exercise of their powers and the discharge of their responsibilities.

### 12. Directors' conflicts of interest

(1) If a proposed decision of the directors is concerned with an actual or proposed transaction or arrangement with the Company in which a director is interested, that director is not to be counted as participating in the decision-making process for quorum or voting purposes.

(2) A director who is interested in a transaction or arrangement with the Company must declare the nature and extent of that interest to the other directors in accordance with sections 177 and 182 of the Act.

(3) The Board may authorise any Conflict proposed to it in accordance with these Articles, subject to the interested director not voting or being counted in the quorum.

### 13. Calling a directors' meeting

(1) Any director may call a directors' meeting by giving notice of the meeting to the directors or by authorising the company secretary (if any) to give such notice.

(2) Notice of any directors' meeting must indicate its proposed date and time, where it is to take place (which may include electronic means), and if it is anticipated that directors participating in the meeting will not be in the same place, how they may participate.

(3) Notice of a directors' meeting must be given to each director not less than five Business Days before the date of the meeting, but a meeting may be called on shorter notice if all directors agree.

### 14. Quorum for directors' meetings

(1) At a directors' meeting, unless a quorum is participating, no proposal is to be voted on except a proposal to call a further meeting.

(2) The quorum for directors' meetings may be fixed from time to time by decision of the directors, but it must never be less than two, unless there is only one director, in which case the quorum shall be one.

(3) If the total number of directors is fewer than the quorum required, the director(s) must not take any decisions other than a decision to appoint further directors or to call a general meeting to enable the members to appoint further directors.

(4) For the purposes of any meeting, a director shall count towards the quorum whether participating in person, by telephone, or by any other electronic means of communication.

### 15. Chairing of directors' meetings

(1) The Founder shall be the Chair of the Board.

(2) If the Founder is not participating in a meeting within ten minutes of the time at which it was to start, the participating directors must appoint one of themselves to chair it.

### 16. Casting vote

(1) Before the issue of Preferred Shares, the Chair shall have a casting vote at Board meetings.

(2) After the issue of Preferred Shares, the Chair shall not have a casting vote unless the Investor Shareholders agree otherwise in writing.

### 17. Directors' written resolutions

(1) A directors' resolution is validly passed when all directors who would have been entitled to vote on it at a Board meeting have signed a copy of it, or otherwise indicated their agreement to it in writing.

(2) A directors' written resolution need not be contained in a single document and may consist of several documents in similar form, each signed by one or more directors.

### 18. Board observer rights

(1) The Investor Majority may appoint one observer to attend and speak at Board meetings. The observer:

- (a) shall receive all Board papers at the same time as directors;
- (b) shall not have a vote;
- (c) may be excluded from any part of a meeting where there is a conflict of interest affecting the Investor Shareholders they represent.

## Part 4 — Reserved Matters

### 19. Matters requiring consent

(1) The Company shall not, and the directors shall not cause or permit the Company to, carry out any Reserved Matter without the prior written consent of:

- (a) before the issue of Preferred Shares: the Founder; and
- (b) after the issue of Preferred Shares: the Investor Majority (or such higher threshold as the terms of the relevant Preferred Shares may require).

(2) The Reserved Matters are set out in Schedule 1 to these Articles.

### 20. Deadlock

(1) A Deadlock exists where the Board is unable to pass a resolution because of the exercise or non-exercise of votes by directors, and such deadlock continues for 30 Business Days after the matter was first proposed to the Board.

(2) On a Deadlock arising, any director may serve a deadlock notice on the Board, requiring the matter to be referred to the respective appointers of the deadlocked directors for resolution in good faith within 20 Business Days.

(3) If the Deadlock is not resolved under (2), either party may refer the matter to mediation under the CEDR Model Mediation Procedure.

## Part 5 — Shares and Share Capital

### 21. Classes of shares

(1) The Company may issue shares in any number of classes, with such rights and restrictions as the directors or the Company by ordinary resolution may determine. Unless specified otherwise, new shares shall be Ordinary Shares.

(2) Preferred Shares shall have such rights as are set out in the terms of issue, which may include but are not limited to:

- (a) a liquidation preference (participating or non-participating);
- (b) anti-dilution protection;
- (c) a right to receive dividends in priority to Ordinary Shares;
- (d) conversion rights into Ordinary Shares;
- (e) information rights; and
- (f) the right to appoint directors and observers under these Articles.

### 22. Issuing shares

(1) Subject to the Articles, but without prejudice to the rights attached to any existing shares, the directors may issue shares with such rights or restrictions as may be determined by ordinary resolution.

(2) The Company shall not issue shares except in accordance with section 551 of the Act (authority to allot) and, where applicable, sections 561-577 of the Act (pre-emption rights) or any disapplication thereof.

### 23. Share certificates

(1) The Company must issue each member with a certificate in respect of the shares registered in their name within two months after allotment or lodgement of a transfer.

(2) Certificates must specify the shares to which they relate and the amount paid up on them.

(3) Share certificates shall be signed by a director and countersigned by a second director or the company secretary.

### 24. Share premium

Any premium received by the Company on the issue of shares shall be credited to a share premium account in accordance with section 610 of the Act.

## Part 6 — Pre-emption on Transfer

### 25. General restriction on transfer

No member shall transfer any share except:

- (a) in accordance with articles 26-38 (pre-emption rights);
- (b) as a Permitted Transfer under article 30;
- (c) pursuant to a drag-along under article 39; or
- (d) pursuant to a tag-along under article 40.

### 26. Pre-emption: transfer notice

(1) A member who wishes to transfer any shares (the **"Seller"**) shall give a Transfer Notice to the Company specifying:

- (a) the number and class of shares proposed to be transferred (the **"Sale Shares"**);
- (b) the price per share at which the Seller wishes to transfer the Sale Shares (the **"Transfer Price"**); and
- (c) the identity of the proposed transferee (if any).

(2) The Transfer Notice constitutes the Company as the Seller's agent for the sale of the Sale Shares in accordance with these Articles.

### 27. Pre-emption: offer to existing members

(1) Within 10 Business Days of receipt of a Transfer Notice, the Company shall give notice (an **"Offer Notice"**) to each other member (other than the Seller) offering the Sale Shares at the Transfer Price.

(2) The Sale Shares shall be offered to each member pro rata to their existing holdings, but any member may apply for more than their pro rata entitlement.

(3) Each member shall have 20 Business Days from the date of the Offer Notice (the **"Acceptance Period"**) to notify the Company in writing whether they wish to acquire any or all of the Sale Shares offered to them.

### 28. Pre-emption: allocation

(1) If applications are received for more than the number of Sale Shares available, they shall be allocated pro rata to existing holdings among the applying members.

(2) If any Sale Shares remain unallocated after pro rata allocation, they shall be offered to those members who applied for more than their pro rata entitlement, in proportion to their existing holdings, until all Sale Shares are allocated or no further applications exist.

### 29. Pre-emption: completion or lapse

(1) If all Sale Shares are taken up, completion shall occur within 10 Business Days after the end of the Acceptance Period.

(2) If not all Sale Shares are taken up, the Seller may, within 60 Business Days after the end of the Acceptance Period, transfer the unsold Sale Shares to any person at a price not less than the Transfer Price and on terms no more favourable than those set out in the Transfer Notice.

(3) If the Seller does not complete a transfer within the 60 Business Day period, the Sale Shares shall again be subject to the pre-emption provisions of these Articles.

### 30. Permitted transfers

(1) A member may transfer shares to a Permitted Transferee at any time without complying with articles 26-29, provided that:

- (a) the transferor gives the Company not less than 5 Business Days' prior written notice;
- (b) the Permitted Transferee enters into a deed of adherence to any shareholders' agreement then in force; and
- (c) if the Permitted Transferee ceases to be a Permitted Transferee of the transferor, the shares are immediately transferred back to the transferor or to another Permitted Transferee.

### 31. Board approval of transfers

(1) The directors may refuse to register any transfer of shares which does not comply with these Articles.

(2) If the directors refuse to register a transfer, they must notify the transferee within two months of receiving the transfer, giving reasons for the refusal.

### 32. Transmission of shares

(1) If a member dies, the only persons recognised by the Company as having title to the deceased member's shares are:

- (a) the personal representatives of the deceased; or
- (b) any person(s) named in a valid transfer notice given by the personal representatives.

(2) Personal representatives may elect either to be registered as holders of the shares or to transfer them to another person. Any such transfer is subject to the pre-emption provisions of these Articles.

## Part 7 — Drag-Along and Tag-Along

### 33. Drag-along

(1) If the holders of 75% or more of the issued share capital (the **"Dragging Shareholders"**) wish to accept a bona fide offer from a third party (who is not a connected person of any Dragging Shareholder) for the entire issued share capital of the Company (an **"Offer"**), the Dragging Shareholders may require all other members (the **"Dragged Shareholders"**) to sell their shares on the same terms and at the same price per share.

(2) The Dragging Shareholders shall give written notice (a **"Drag Notice"**) to the Dragged Shareholders specifying:

- (a) the identity of the proposed buyer;
- (b) the consideration per share;
- (c) any other material terms;
- (d) the proposed completion date (not less than 15 Business Days from the Drag Notice).

(3) On receipt of a Drag Notice, each Dragged Shareholder shall:

- (a) transfer their shares to the buyer on the terms set out in the Drag Notice; and
- (b) execute all documents and take all steps reasonably necessary to complete the sale.

(4) If a Dragged Shareholder fails to comply with (3) within 5 Business Days of the proposed completion date, any director is irrevocably appointed as the Dragged Shareholder's attorney to execute the transfer on their behalf.

(5) The consideration received by each Dragged Shareholder shall reflect the same price per share and form of consideration as that received by the Dragging Shareholders, adjusted only for differences in class rights (including liquidation preferences).

### 34. Tag-along

(1) If one or more members (the **"Selling Shareholders"**) propose to transfer in aggregate more than 50% of the issued share capital to a third party (who is not a Permitted Transferee), each other member (a **"Tagging Shareholder"**) shall have the right, but not the obligation, to require the buyer to purchase a proportionate number of the Tagging Shareholder's shares on the same terms.

(2) The Selling Shareholders shall give written notice (a **"Tag Notice"**) to the Company and all other members not less than 20 Business Days before the proposed completion date, containing the same information as a Drag Notice.

(3) Each Tagging Shareholder shall have 15 Business Days from receipt of the Tag Notice to notify the Selling Shareholders in writing that they wish to exercise their tag-along right.

(4) If any Tagging Shareholder exercises their right, the Selling Shareholders shall procure that the buyer purchases the Tagging Shareholder's shares on the same terms and at the same price per share as the Selling Shareholders' shares, adjusted only for differences in class rights.

(5) If the buyer refuses to purchase the Tagging Shareholders' shares, the Selling Shareholders shall not complete the proposed transfer.

## Part 8 — Vesting and Leaver Provisions

### 35. Founder vesting

(1) The Ordinary Shares held by the Founder as at the date of the Company's first funding round resulting in the issue of Preferred Shares shall be subject to vesting as follows:

- (a) 25% shall vest immediately on completion of the funding round (the **"Initial Vesting"**);
- (b) the remaining 75% shall vest in equal monthly instalments over the following 36 months (the **"Vesting Period"**);
- (c) vesting is conditional on the Founder remaining employed by or engaged as a director of the Company.

(2) The Board may, by unanimous resolution, accelerate vesting in whole or in part.

(3) On a Change of Control (being a sale of more than 50% of the issued share capital or a sale of substantially all assets), all unvested shares shall vest immediately.

### 36. Leaver

A **"Leaver"** is a person who ceases to be both an employee and a director of the Company, whether voluntarily or involuntarily.

### 37. Good Leaver

A person is a **"Good Leaver"** if they become a Leaver as a result of:

- (a) death;
- (b) permanent incapacity (as certified by a registered medical practitioner);
- (c) termination by the Company without Cause;
- (d) resignation with the prior written agreement of the Board that the person shall be treated as a Good Leaver; or
- (e) the Company undergoing a material breach of the Leaver's service agreement which is not remedied within 30 Business Days of written notice.

### 38. Bad Leaver

A person is a **"Bad Leaver"** if they become a Leaver other than as a Good Leaver, including but not limited to:

- (a) voluntary resignation (other than under article 37(d) or 37(e));
- (b) termination for Cause. **"Cause"** means:
  - (i) a material breach of the person's service agreement;
  - (ii) commission of an act of fraud, dishonesty, or gross misconduct;
  - (iii) conviction of a criminal offence (other than a road traffic offence not resulting in imprisonment);
  - (iv) a material breach of the person's fiduciary duties to the Company.

### 39. Consequences of leaving — unvested shares

(1) On a Leaver ceasing to hold office:

- (a) all unvested shares held by the Leaver shall be transferred as follows:
  - (i) if the Leaver is a Good Leaver: at Fair Market Value (as determined under article 42);
  - (ii) if the Leaver is a Bad Leaver: at the lower of the original subscription price paid and the nominal value of the shares;

- (b) the Leaver is deemed to have served a Transfer Notice in respect of all unvested shares at the relevant price, and the provisions of articles 26-29 shall apply as if the Transfer Notice had been given on the date the person became a Leaver.

(2) Vested shares held by a Leaver remain subject to the pre-emption provisions of these Articles but are not subject to compulsory transfer.

### 40. Fair Market Value

(1) The **"Fair Market Value"** of a share is the price per share agreed between the Leaver and the Board or, failing agreement within 15 Business Days, the price per share as determined by an independent valuer.

(2) The independent valuer shall be a chartered accountant appointed by agreement between the Leaver and the Board, or failing agreement within 10 Business Days, by the President for the time being of the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales.

(3) The independent valuer shall act as an expert and not as an arbitrator. Their determination shall be final and binding (absent manifest error).

(4) The costs of the independent valuer shall be borne equally by the Company and the Leaver.

## Part 9 — General Meetings and Resolutions

### 41. Calling general meetings

(1) The directors may call a general meeting at any time.

(2) The directors must call a general meeting if required to do so by the members under section 303 of the Act.

(3) General meetings shall be called by not less than 14 clear days' notice (or such shorter period as the Act permits with the agreement of the requisite majority).

### 42. Quorum for general meetings

(1) No business other than the appointment of the chair of the meeting is to be transacted at a general meeting if the persons attending it do not constitute a quorum.

(2) A quorum shall be one qualifying person present in person or by proxy, provided that:

- (a) after the issue of Preferred Shares, the quorum shall include at least one Investor Shareholder (or their proxy); and
- (b) if the Company has more than one member, the quorum shall be at least two qualifying persons.

### 43. Attendance and speaking at general meetings

(1) A person is able to exercise the right to speak at a general meeting when that person is in a position to communicate to all those attending the meeting, during the meeting, any information or opinions which that person has on the business of the meeting.

(2) A person is able to exercise the right to vote at a general meeting when they are able to vote and have their vote counted at the same time as the votes of all other persons attending the meeting.

(3) The directors may make whatever arrangements they consider appropriate to enable those attending a general meeting to exercise their rights, including participation by electronic means.

### 44. Votes of members

(1) On a vote on a resolution on a show of hands, each member present in person or by proxy shall have one vote.

(2) On a vote on a resolution on a poll, every member shall have one vote for each share held.

### 45. Proxies

(1) A member is entitled to appoint another person as their proxy to exercise all or any of their rights to attend, speak, and vote at a general meeting.

(2) Proxies may only validly be appointed by a notice in writing (a **"proxy notice"**) which states the name and address of the member, identifies the proxy, and is signed by or on behalf of the member.

### 46. Written resolutions

(1) A resolution in writing agreed by members holding the requisite majority shall be as effective as if passed at a general meeting.

(2) A written ordinary resolution requires agreement by members holding more than 50% of the total voting rights.

(3) A written special resolution requires agreement by members holding 75% or more of the total voting rights.

## Part 10 — Dividends and Distributions

### 47. Dividends

(1) The Company may by ordinary resolution declare dividends, and the directors may decide to pay interim dividends.

(2) No dividend may be declared or paid unless it is in accordance with members' respective rights and the directors' recommendation.

(3) No dividend may be declared or paid unless the directors have made a recommendation as to its amount. Such a dividend may not exceed the amount recommended by the directors.

(4) No dividend may be declared or paid unless the Company has sufficient distributable profits.

(5) Unless the rights of a class of shares provide otherwise, dividends shall be declared and paid on Ordinary Shares and Preferred Shares in the order specified by the terms of issue of the Preferred Shares.

### 48. Payment of dividends

(1) Where a dividend is to be paid in cash, it must be paid by bank transfer to the account specified by the member.

(2) The Company may deduct from any dividend payable to a member any sums presently payable by the member to the Company.

## Part 11 — Winding Up and Liquidation Preference

### 49. Distribution on winding up

(1) On a return of capital on winding up or otherwise (other than a conversion, redemption, or purchase of shares), the assets available for distribution shall be applied in the following order:

- (a) **First**, in paying to the holders of Preferred Shares an amount equal to the higher of:
  - (i) the aggregate subscription price paid for those Preferred Shares, plus any declared but unpaid dividends (**"Liquidation Preference"**); or
  - (ii) the amount those holders would receive if the Preferred Shares had been converted to Ordinary Shares immediately before the return of capital;

- (b) **Second**, the balance (if any) shall be distributed among the holders of Ordinary Shares pro rata to their holdings.

(2) This article is subject to the terms of issue of any Preferred Shares, which may specify a participating or non-participating liquidation preference or a multiple of the subscription price.

## Part 12 — Anti-dilution

### 50. Anti-dilution protection

(1) If the Company issues shares at a price per share less than the price paid by the holders of Preferred Shares in the most recent funding round (a **"Down Round"**), the holders of Preferred Shares shall be entitled to anti-dilution protection on a broad-based weighted average basis, unless the terms of issue specify otherwise.

(2) Anti-dilution protection shall be implemented by adjusting the conversion ratio at which Preferred Shares convert to Ordinary Shares, rather than by the issue of additional shares.

(3) The following issuances are excluded from anti-dilution adjustments:

- (a) shares issued under an employee share option scheme approved by the Board;
- (b) shares issued on conversion of convertible loan notes;
- (c) shares issued as consideration for the acquisition of another company or business;
- (d) shares issued with the consent of the Investor Majority.

---

## Part 13 — Information Rights

### 51. Information rights

(1) Each Investor Shareholder (or group of Investor Shareholders acting together) holding at least 5% of the Company's issued share capital shall be entitled to receive:

- (a) unaudited monthly management accounts within 30 days of month end;
- (b) annual audited accounts (once the Company is required to prepare them);
- (c) an annual budget and business plan;
- (d) prompt notification of any event that is material to the Company's business, financial condition, or prospects.

(2) The Company shall maintain proper books and records in accordance with the Act.

(3) Information provided under this article shall be subject to the confidentiality obligations of any shareholders' agreement then in force.

---

## Part 14 — Conversion Rights

### 52. Conversion of Preferred Shares

(1) Each holder of Preferred Shares may convert some or all of their Preferred Shares into Ordinary Shares at any time by giving written notice to the Company.

(2) The initial conversion ratio shall be 1:1 (one Preferred Share converts to one Ordinary Share), subject to adjustment for:

- (a) any anti-dilution adjustment under article 50;
- (b) any subdivision, consolidation, or reorganisation of the share capital.

(3) All Preferred Shares shall automatically convert to Ordinary Shares on:

- (a) an IPO; or
- (b) the written election of the Investor Majority.

## Part 15 — Miscellaneous

### 53. Indemnity and insurance

(1) Subject to section 232 of the Act, the Company may indemnify any director or former director against any liability incurred in connection with any negligence, default, breach of duty, or breach of trust in relation to the Company, to the extent permitted by sections 232-235 of the Act.

(2) The directors may purchase and maintain insurance, at the expense of the Company, for the benefit of any director or former director against any liability incurred in connection with their duties as a director.

### 54. Means of communication

(1) Any notice or document may be sent to a member by electronic means to the address the member has provided for that purpose.

(2) A member who has not provided an electronic address shall be entitled to receive notices and documents by post.

### 55. Company secretary

The directors may appoint and remove a company secretary, and shall do so if required by the Act.

### 56. Seal

If the Company has a seal, it shall only be used by the authority of the directors or of a committee authorised by the directors.

### 57. Governing law

These Articles shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales. The courts of England and Wales shall have exclusive jurisdiction in relation to any dispute arising out of or in connection with these Articles.
